References

The sources below were used to research search engine optimization, technical SEO, website performance, structured data, and SEO best practices.

  1. Google Search Central. (n.d.). Search Engine Optimization (SEO) Starter Guide. Google for Developers. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide
  2. Google Search Central. (n.d.). Google Search Essentials. Google for Developers. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials
  3. Google Search Central. (n.d.). Google Search Technical Requirements. Google for Developers. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/technical
  4. Google Search Central. (n.d.). Build and Submit a Sitemap. Google for Developers. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/sitemaps/build-sitemap
  5. Google Search Central. (n.d.). Introduction to Structured Data Markup in Google Search. Google for Developers. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/intro-structured-data
  6. Google Search Central. (n.d.). Image SEO Best Practices. Google for Developers. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/google-images
  7. web.dev. (n.d.). Web Vitals. Google. https://web.dev/articles/vitals
  8. Google. (n.d.). PageSpeed Insights. https://pagespeed.web.dev/
  9. Microsoft Bing. (n.d.). Bing Webmaster Tools Help Documentation. Bing Webmaster Tools. https://www.bing.com/webmasters/help/webmaster-guidelines-30fba23a
  10. Ahrefs. (n.d.). SEO Basics: Beginner’s Guide to SEO Success. https://ahrefs.com/seo/seo-basics

Helpful Learning Resources

These references are useful because they explain SEO from both a search engine side and a website owner side. Google Search Central is helpful for understanding how Google reads and indexes websites. web.dev and PageSpeed Insights are helpful for performance, while Ahrefs gives an explanation of SEO strategy.
